Arnala Fort, Vasai

Palghar

Coastal Fort, Built in 15th Century

History about location

Arnala Fort, also known as Jaldurg, is a coastal fort located in Arnala, a town in the outskirts of Vasai in the Palghar district of Maharashtra, India. The fort was built during the 15th century by the Sultan of Gujarat. It was later captured by the Portuguese in 1532 and served as a strategic location for trade and defense. Today, Arnala Fort stands as a popular tourist attraction, offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea.

Visit Arnala Fort

Arnala Fort is best visited during the winter months from November to February when the weather is pleasant. You can reach Arnala Fort by road from Mumbai or Vasai. It's approximately a 2-hour drive from Mumbai. Alternatively, you can take a train to Vasai and then a short cab ride to Arnala.

Facilities

Arnala Fort offers basic facilities such as washrooms and drinking water. However, it is advisable to carry your own food and water as there are limited options available within the fort premises. Additionally, there are no accommodation facilities within the fort, but there are nearby hotels and resorts in Arnala for overnight stays.

Precautions

It's important to take precautions while visiting Arnala Fort to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience:

  • Wear sturdy footwear as the terrain can be uneven.
  • Carry sufficient water and snacks as there are limited refreshment options.
  • Be cautious while exploring the fort, especially around the edges.
  • Respect the historical significance of the fort and avoid littering.
